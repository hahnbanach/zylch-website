Architecture

Neurosymbolic Design

Zylch uses a neurosymbolic architecture—combining neural networks (LLMs) for understanding with symbolic systems (rules, logic) for reliable execution.

You: "Remind Sofia about the demo"
↓
LLM Layer: Understands intent, resolves "Sofia"
↓
Memory Layer: Retrieves Sofia's Avatar, preferences
↓
Symbolic Layer: Creates rule: IF tomorrow THEN remind
↓
Execution: Guaranteed delivery (email, WhatsApp, call)

Why LLMs Alone Don't Work

Large Language Models are remarkable at understanding and generating text. But for business work, they have fundamental limitations:

No Memory

LLMs forget everything between sessions. They can't remember "I told you this yesterday."

No State

They can't track "wait for response, then do X." Every request starts fresh.

No Reliability

Same prompt, different results. You can't build business processes on probabilistic outputs.

No Action

They generate text, not results. "Send an email" produces words about emails, not sent emails.

Zylch solves each of these: Our memory system persists knowledge. Our symbolic layer tracks state. Our rule engine guarantees execution. Our integrations take real action.
